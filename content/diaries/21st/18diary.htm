1918

January 1st = 1918. New=20 Year's Day. Went out to Richmond and in the evening saw "Aladdin" at = Drury Lane.=20 It started at 7.30 pm and I left at midnight. They were just starting on = the=20 second last scene.

January 2nd = 1918. Oh !=20 Disaster. Leave was over and I returned to camp.

January 3rd = 1918. Heavy=20 frosts again and as cold as can be.

January 4th = 1918. It snowed=20 again today. Cold, well it is beyond all words.

January 5th = 1918. Snowing=20 again today.

January 6th = 1918. Had a=20 heavy frost last night.

January 7th = 1918. It rained=20 all day. My birthday too. ( 21st. )

January 8th = 1918. Frosty=20 again.

January 9th to 12th = 1918.=20 Nothing doing.

January 13th = 1918. Nothing=20 doing but one great event. Your much welcomed parcel landed. The = puddings were=20 A1. Had quite a feed.

January 14th = 1918. A very=20 heavy fall of snow last night. It was registered as 18 inches. Some fall = eh?=20 Five of us instructors challenged an even of men in the school to a snow = fight=20 but before long we had an attack from 300 men. It was a tussle I can = tell=20 you.

January 15th = 1918. It=20 rained heavily, so when we woke up there was not a sign of yesterdays=20 fall.

January 16th = 1918. Still=20 raining heavily.

January 17th = 1918. Last=20 night we had another 6 inches of snow. Sleet all day. Things are that = mucky now=20 one does not know where to go.

January 18th to 23rd = 1918.=20 Nothing important.

January 24th and 25th = 1918.=20 I have been decorating preparatory to our dance which is coming off = tomorrow=20 night.

January 26th = 1918. Our mess=20 dance tonight was a great success and quite enjoyed myself.

January 27th = 1918. Woke up=20 tired and weary. Did not get to bed till 4.30 am. this morning. In the = afternoon=20 we went down to see the Fovant Church. It is an old building.

January 28th = 1918. Raining=20 again.

January 29th = 1918. About=20 half our staff went on draft to France today. I expect my turn will be=20 next.

January 30th to = February 4th=20 1918. Rained nearly all these days.

February 5th = 1918. There=20 was a sudden mobilisation today.

February 6th to 11th = 1918.=20 We have had cold and stormy weather.

February 12th = 1918. Had a=20 mess meeting tonight. I was appointed Secretary of the Amusement = Committee for a=20 whist drive and dance to be held in about a fortnight's time.

February 13th to 17th = 1918.=20 We spent these nights painting the mess preparatory to the Whist Drive.=20

February 18th to 20th = 1918.=20 Some lovely fine days.

February 21st to 28th.=20 1918. Nothing to report.

March 1st 1918. = I went on=20 leave to London arriving at 3 pm. In the evening we went to the "Beauty = Spot".=20 It was a lovely play and the dancing was beautiful.

March 2nd 1918. = Spent the=20 day in London. In the evening we saw "Arlette".

March 3rd 1918. = Went to=20 Ciro's Y.M.C.A. in the afternoon and the Regent Palace in the=20 evening.

March 4th 1918. = The big=20 Tank was today opened in Trafalgar Square. They also have the Gondola of = a Zepp.=20 These things were acting as Banks for the War Bond campaign. In the = evening we=20 went to the Alhambra to see the "Bing Boys on Broadway".

March 5th 1918. = In the=20 morning I went round the City ( proper ) of London. It is absolutely = marvellous=20 up this way. Saw the Bank of England, Royal Exchange and Mansion House = where I=20 saw the Lord Mayor out in all his regalia.

In the afternoon we went to = the House of=20 Commons, Lloyd George, Sir Eric Geddes and Asquith spoke on the Naval = situation.=20 It was very interesting. In the evening we went to the Grand Opera at = Drury Lane=20 and saw "Faust". Spent the night at my mate's place at = Camberwell.

March 6th 1918. = Returned to=20 camp today.

March 7th, 8th and 9th=20 1918. Nothing important.

March 10th = 1918. Went for a=20 walk to Teffont Manor. This is a very fine old house with some lovely=20 scenery.

March 11th to 15th = 1918.=20 Thank goodness we have had some lovely weather here lately.

March 16th = 1918. We went=20 down to Wilton and saw the very old and historical residence of Wilton = House,=20 the seat of the Earl of Pembroke. It was something glorious.

March 17th to 21st = 1918.=20 There is a terrible bombardment in progress in France. Once more it is=20 fine.

March 22nd = 1918. News came=20 today of an enormous German offensive on the Arras, St. Quenten=20 Sector.

March 23rd = 1918. Went for a=20 wander around the hills near here and explored an old Roman Camp and = Roman Road=20 dating from 53 B.C.

March 24th = 1918. Went for a=20 wander in the nearby woods. The trees and flowers are getting lovely=20 now.

March 25th = 1918. News came=20 through of the fall of Bapaume, Peronne and Ham, all the places for = which we=20 fought hard. The situation and strain here in England is = terrible.

I was put on some very = special and secret=20 work today.

March 26th = 1918. News of=20 the fall of Noyon and Nesle. The position is getting serious.

March 27th = 1918. Roy and=20 Rosieres fell yesterday. The strain here is worse than at the outbreak = of=20 War.

March 28th = 1918. Situation=20 very critical. The Bosch has all the ground around Pozieres, for which = so many=20 of our boys have made the supreme sacrifice. Albert, Bray and Montdidier = fell=20 yesterday.

March 29th = 1918. Better=20 news today. The enemy who is trying for Amiens, has been suddenly=20 stopped.

March 30th = 1918. Still the=20 news is better. Only a slight advance was made by him yesterday. We had = our=20 dance tonight. It was something lovely.

March 31st = 1918. We were=20 dancing until 4.45 am this morning. In the evening we went for a wander = in the=20 woods. The primroses etc are lovely. The fields and woods are now a mass = of=20 yellow with these and daffodils.

April 1st to 5th = 1918.=20 Nothing doing only a great spell of weather, fine in the morning and the = opposite in the evening.

April 6th 1918. = The great=20 battle in France has once more resumed. In the evening, we went for a = walk to=20 Sutton, Mandeville, a pretty little place about 2 miles from here. Then = we=20 climbed a big hill some 700 feet high and explored some remains of old = Roman=20 Water Ditch and Road which only of late years dropped out of = use.

April 7th 1918. = Rather wet=20 and cold. We took a walk down to Compton-Chamberlain, a small village = about 1=20 1/2 miles from here. It is a little hovel ( very quaint ) which = straggles=20 amongst a couple of insignificant valleys with awful odours.

Coming home, we struck a path = through the=20 woods. Here the flowers, primroses, daffodil, wood anenomies and violets = (=20 Purple and

white ) were growing in great = profusion.=20 The scent was lovely and the song of the birds in the air, made one = dream of=20 that bonny place that seems to get further away as time goes on,=20 Australia.

April 9th 1918. = Out all day=20 on a big Signal scheme. Had rather a good time. The hills over which we = are=20 working are just covered with violets.

April 10th = 1918. News came=20 today of another big battle near Armentieres.

April 11th = 1918. The Bosch=20 is still advancing. News of the fall of Armentiers, Bois Grenier, = Fleurbaix and=20 Ploegsteert came through today. They are all old hunting grounds of=20 ours.

April 12th = 1918. Another=20 all day Signal Scheme.

April 13th = 1918. Went for a=20 walk via Fovant, Sutton-Mandeville to Wardour Castle and back home via = Anstey,=20 Swallowcliffe and home.

Wardour Castle is a fine = ruined old=20 castle in enormous grounds in which the scenery is simply marvellous. = Also near=20 here is the new castle, a very ordinary building. To come back to the = old one.=20 This was the seat of Lady Arundel and in Cromwellian days, the followers = of=20 Cromwell besieged the place for about 4 days, after which the owners had = to=20 surrender. The castle is surrounded on three sides by hills and in front = by a=20 lake, so one can see how the siege was conducted.

In the walls now, are the = bullet holes=20 and the holes where cannon balls struck it. Surrounding the place are = some very=20 fine cedars and Rhodadendrums. I have a nice set of photos so you can = see for=20 yourself.

April 14th to 18th = 1918.=20 Spent all these days decorating a hall for our Brigade Officer's Dance.=20

April 19th = 1918. Our Mess=20 held a smoke night and musical evening. Had a very nice time.

April 20th = 1918. Worked all=20 day on the Officer's hall. In the evening I was invited to supper and = had a few=20 dances. They are very pleased with the hall.

April 21st = 1918. Arrived=20 home this morning at 4 am. It was a glorious day; but, being tired, I = slept=20 nearly all day. The Commandant was that pleased with the ball, he = granted me 7=20 days leave.

April 22nd to 24th = 1918.=20 Spent all these days decorating another bigger hall for another officers = dance=20 night. Was also invited to this and had a really tip top = time.

April 25th = 1918. Landed=20 home at 5.30 am. this morning so did not get to bed. Got my things ready = for=20 leave and caught the midday train to London. I stayed the night at the = Strand=20 Palace Hotel. In the evening I went to see "Yes Uncle", a comic opera. = It was=20 lovely.

April 26th = 1918. Went to=20 see Miss Henning today and stayed the night. They are such nice people.=20

April 27th = 1918. Went out=20 to see Mrs. Morrison. Had another lovely day.

April 28th = 1918. Went out=20 to my mates place at Waddon. Had a real good time.

April 29th = 1918. Went round=20 London at will and viewed some of the damage done by Zepps and air = raids. Better=20 not say too much about this or the censor may object. In the evening I = went to=20 see the play "Nothing but the truth" at the Savoy. It was the funniest = thing I=20 have ever seen.

April 30th = 1918. Went out=20 to Weddon again. Had a fine day and musical evening. Quite enjoyed=20 it.

May 1st 1918. = Went to the=20 National Service Museum. Here they have many carefully preserved and = interesting=20 relics. The building is the old Banqueting Hall and all that is left of = the old=20 Whitehall Palace built by James 1st. It was through this hall that = Charles 1st=20 went to be beheaded in the public thoroughfare in front. For a time = Cromwell=20 reigned supreme but was ejected.

Amongst the most interesting = relics are=20 the following :-

Napoleon's Razor.

Napoleon's Chair form his = chamber at St.=20 Helena.

Table on which the Treaty of = Sebastopol=20 was signed.

Duke of Wellington's Hat and = Uniform,=20 besides those ( some actually blood stained with their life blood ) of = other=20 famous soldiers.

Pieces of old and historic = boats, Guns=20 and War Trophies hundreds of years old, The first Machine gun, the = Skeleton of=20 Napoleon's Favourite Charger, Saddle used by Bluecher at Waterloo, = actual=20 standard of "The Commonwealth of England" numerous relics of Nelson = including=20 Hat, Uniform, Writing Tablet, Pens etc. There are many other very = interesting=20 things which would take to long to explain, here.

May 2nd 1918. = Returned to=20 camp today, and as soon as I landed was detailed for night operations. = Unlucky=20 me eh !

May 3rd and 4th = 1918.=20 Feeling very off colour today. Had a return of the old trench fever and = running=20 a very high temperature. My arm broke out again in two = places.

May 5th to 11th = 1918. Cold=20 and wet.

May 12th 1918. = I went for a=20 stroll in the woods today. Well, words will never describe it. the trees = are now=20 literally twined into one dense mass with honeysuckle. Then the trees = themselves=20 are clothed in every shade of green imaginable. The ground is one dense = carpet=20 of violets, blue bells, cowslips, daffodils and wood = anemones.

These are not just scattered = here and=20 there but one huge and glorious blaze. To finish up this absolute = heaven, the=20 birds in the trees are singing a glorious medley. It makes one feel = quite=20 pleased to be alive.

May 13th and 14th = 1918. Wet=20 again.

May 15th to 17th = 1918. Some=20 Bonza days, just like Aussie days.

May 18th 1918. = Went to=20 Dinton for tea and to the pictures in the evening.

May 19th 1918. = My mate went=20 to France today. It was awful hot.

May 20th to 27th = 1918. All=20 very fine and hot days.

May 28th 1918. = I was today=20 made storeman of the Signal School and am now in charge of 2,500 pounds = worth of=20 material. Some job eh!

May 29th 1918. = The job I=20 got yesterday was only temporary as I volunteered for France today so am = awaiting draft.

May 30th and 31st = 1918.=20 Still warm and fine days.

June 1st 1918. = Another big=20 battle is now raging in France. This time it is a direct drive for=20 Paris.

June 2nd 1918. = Terribly hot=20 all day. Went to the pictures this evening. They are awful. Beyond=20 words.

June 3rd to 5th = 1918.=20 Nothing doing other than the old routine.

June 6th 1918. = About 7 of=20 us clubbed together and had a little farewell supper tonight up at the = school.=20 During the last couple of days I have been repairing a Gramophone, so we = got the=20 lend of about 100 records and I can tell you we had a real good = time.

June 7th to 11th = 1918.=20 Nothing other than monotonous routine of camp life. I am getting = heartily sick=20 of this and food is awful beyond words.

June 12th and 13th = 1918.=20 Spent both these days decorating another hall for an Officers dance. = Tonight our=20 unit held a boxing tournament. It was a rather fine show too.

June 14th 1918. = I was down=20 in the village of Fovant having supper when a chap came up to me and = asked me if=20 my name was Williams. On assuring him that such it was so, he informed = me he was=20 none other than Ted Dubois.

June 15th and 16th = 1918.=20 Spent in decorating Mess. Raining again.

June 17th 1918. = Worked hard=20 all day finishing off for the dance tonight. It looked lovely. In the = evening I=20 was in charge of the bar. Took 85 pounds. The hall looks tres bon. I = have done=20 it in heliotrope and green. They blend very well.

June 18th 1918. = 5.30 am.=20 Just landed home tired as can be.

June 19th and 20th = 1918.=20 Nothing of interest.

June 21st 1918. = On looking=20 back through the diary I have not yet given you an idea of our daily = routine, so=20 will give it now for the weekend.

Reveille at 6 am, Breakfast = at 6.40 am. (=20 Rotten ) Company Parade at 7.20 am. Signal School Parade at 7.50 am. to = noon.=20 Dinner 12.15 pm ( Awful ) Company Parade 1.15 pm. Signal School Parade = 1.45 pm.=20 to 4 pm. Tea at 5 pm.

Voluntary Parade on which I = must always=20 be. 6 to 7 pm. The rest of the night to ourselves up to 9 pm. lights=20 out.

June 22nd 1918. = Reveille 6=20 am. Breakfast 6.30 am. Company Parade full packs 7.15 am. Battalion = Parade 8 am.=20 for a 15 mile route march to about 1 pm. The remainder of the day our=20 own.

June 23rd 1918. = Reveille,=20 6.30 am. Breakfast 7 am. Church Parade 8.30 am. Dinner noon. ( Rotten = again. ).=20 Rest of the day clear. This afternoon Wal came over from Hetesbury. We = had quite=20 a good old yarn and went to the pictures in the evening.

June 24th 1918. = Went to the=20 pictures this evening.

June 25th 1918. = Good but=20 vague news from the Italian Front. It appears as if they have cut off = large=20 portions of the Austrian Army which is on the run.

June 26th 1918. = Woke up=20 this morning feeling pretty crook. Went to see doctor. My temperature = was 104.8=20 not too bad eh! Result, I was, with many others bundled off to hospital. = By=20 about 11 am. over 600 of the camp of 750 were effected. It was the=20 flu.

June 27th to 30th = 1918. All=20 these days were spent in Hospital. Feeling much better today.

July 1st to 3rd = 1918. Still=20 in hospital and feeling rather groggy.

July 4th 1918. = I was=20 discharged from this infernal hole and given 3 days leave in = London.

July 5th 1918. = Left for=20 London at 4.15 pm. arrived there about 8 pm. and went out to Miss. = Hennings for=20 the night.

July 6th 1918. = Went into=20 the city in the morning. It was a lovely day. In the evening went to the = opera=20 at Drury Lane and saw "Carmen". This was just beautiful. The singing was = all=20 that could be desired.

July 7th 1918. = Went to=20 Richmond and spent the day on the river. Quite enjoyed = myself.

July 8th 1918. = Went to=20 Hampton Court today. This is a fine old palace of Henry VIII., all of = red brick=20 and white cement. Of the hundred or so chimney pots, there is not two = the same=20 design.

Here there is a vine hundreds = of years=20 old and the trunk of which is far bigger round than me and I'm not = small. It is=20 a bonza place. In the evening we went to the opera again and saw "Aida". = Of all=20 the glorious opera's I have seen, this is the best. It was gorgeously = staged and=20 the singing was just lovely. The part of "Aida" was taken by Madam Elsa = Stralia,=20 the famous Australian Singer. She was just it.

July 9th 1918. = Returned=20 home again today.

July 10th and 11th = 1918.=20 Raining heavens hard. In fact today is like the middle of winter=20 again.

July 12th to 15th = 1918.=20 Still raining. This is getting very serious as all the crops are = beginning to=20 rot and grow again. As if the food situation was not bad enough as it=20 is.

July 16th 1918. = War Weapons=20 Day in Tisbury. They held sports. We all went out and had a real good=20 time.

July 17th to 24th = 1918.=20 Nothing happened although the big counter offensive is still in progress = and=20 looks promising.

July 25th and 26th = 1918.=20 Went to Tisbury today and had a very nice time.

July 27th 1918. = Nothing=20 doing.

July 28th 1918. = Awfully=20 hot. I went out to Wardour Castle and had quite a lovely day. Coming = home I rode=20 10 miles on the back of my mates bike. It was great fun but very=20 tiring.

July 29th 1918. = Nothing to=20 report.

July 30th 1918. = I was=20 warned for France today after parading about twice a day foe three weeks = to be=20 put on draft.

July 31st 1918. = Went=20 through the Doctor and Dentist today. Both marked me fit. So Goodbye on=20 Sunday.

August 1st and 2nd = 1918.=20 Nothing to report. Just been told the draft is not leaving till=20 Wednesday.

August 3rd = 1918. Went to=20 Salisbury today. Had a great time. In the evening we went to the=20 theatre.

August 4th = 1918. War=20 Remembrance Day. Had a big Church Parade. In the afternoon we went to = Wardour=20 Castle but it rained, so got a wetting.

August 5th = 1918. The=20 cheerful war news still continues.

August 6th = 1918. This=20 morning's paper still gives the great news, the Hun is retiring in four=20 different places.

August 7th = 1918. We fell in=20 at 6 pm. and marched to Dinton, entrained for Folkestone at 8.15 = pm.

August 8th = 1918. 3.15 pm.=20 arrived at Shorncliff and marched to Folkestone where we went into = billets for a=20 short sleep till 9.30 am. then embarked for Boulogne arriving there = after a good=20 trip across at 12.30 pm. It was rather cold, but en route we passed the = spot=20 where the Hospital ship "Warilda" was sunk by submarines without = warning. The=20 spot is now marked with buoys. In the afternoon and evening, we went = into=20 Boulogne and had a ripping time.

There is rather a beautiful = Church here=20 after the style ( in architecture ) of the Melbourne Exhibition=20 Buildings.

August 9th = 1918. A glorious=20 day. News came through of another big Allied success at Montdidier. At = 10 am. we=20 fell in, marched to the station and entrained at 1 pm. for Le Havre, = proceeded=20 via Etaples, Abbeyville and Rouen arriving there at midnight but stopped = at the=20 station till morning. En route, at Etaples we saw the big Hospitals = which had=20 been completely destroyed when Fritz had his big bombing raid. =

The scenery, en route, is = just lovely and=20 there are several very large tunnels near Rouen.

August 10th = 1918. The good=20 news from the front still continues. 4 am. the train started again ( = From Rouen=20 ) and we continued our journey to Le Havre, arriving there at 11 am. It = is=20 awfully hot but a fine camp and good food. Just before we left Rouen, a = Zepp.=20 passed over head and dropped four or five very large bombs close at = hand. In the=20 evening, we went to the Cinema provided free by the Comforts Fund for = the=20 troops.

August 11th = 1918. Nothing=20 to do all day other than a medical inspection. During the morning, we = marched to=20 Harfleur and went through Gas to test our masks. Still awfully hot. In = the=20 afternoon we went for a walk around the forest and other places round = here. It=20 is very pretty.

The evening we spent at an = open air=20 service held by the finest Padre I have met. He is commonly known as = "The Father=20 Of the Aussies".

August 12th = 1918. We were=20 warned to go further. At 1 pm. we fell in and marched to Le Havre ( a = distance=20 of 6 miles ). The march was awfully hot. At 8 pm. we entrained and moved = off at=20 8.30 pm.

August 13th = 1918. After a=20 very pretty trip, we arrived at Pernois and marched to the rest camp at=20 Bertaucourt. I was here some two years ago and many of the people = remembered=20 me.

After we got settled down, we = were shot=20 off to go through gas once more. Then the best part, we went for a swim = in the=20 river. The camp is pitched in a wood on this river so the afternoon was = well=20 spent.

August 14th = 1918. 9 am. We=20 were warned for our units tomorrow. During the day we had organised = games and=20 had some great fun with the French kiddies. In the evening we went for a = walk=20 down the village.

August 15th = 1918. We=20 marched to Vignacourt which is quite a large village. Here we took the = light=20 railway for Allenville. From here we marched to Lamotte on the Somme = Canal. At=20 night we had a big raid ( air ) by Fritz. The bombs dropped very = uncomfortably=20 near.

August 16th = 1918. Went for=20 a walk across to Glisy. It was very pretty walking over the old corded = roads=20 through the swamps of the Somme. The town has suffered considerably from = gun-fire. From here we can see Amiens and the famous Villers-Bretonneux. = In the=20 afternoon, we watched them dragging for a couple of chaps who had been=20 drowned.

August 17th = 1918. Nothing=20 interesting. Parade as usual. Had another air raid from = Fritz.

August = 18th1918. Fine.=20 Nothing interesting.

August 19th = 1918. We=20 marched to Dauors and joined our Battalion.

August 20th = 1918. Fine. We=20 rested all day. In the morning had a bath in the Canal and in the = afternoon went=20 through the village which has suffered from shell fire.

August 21st = 1918. Very=20 foggy. The Battalion reorganised. Another raid by Fritz. The bombs = rather close=20 again.

August 22nd = 1918. Walked=20 across to Bussy-les-Daurs. Good news came through today.

August 23rd = 1918. Went into=20 Dauors for a walk. It has been very hot and exceptionally heavy = bombardment has=20 been going on all day. We had a demonstration of the new signal rocket. = There=20 have been hundreds of wounded passing through all day. In the evening we = walked=20 to Aubigny for a stroll.

August 24th = 1918. Rifle=20 inspection in the morning. Went for a walk to Bussy-les-Dauora in the = afternoon=20 and saw the Second Divisional Concert Party.

When coming home at 9.30 pm = we were just=20 in time to catch a big air raid from Fritz. Some of the bombs dropped = right in=20 the village.

August 25th = 1918. "Stood=20 to" all day. 8 pm. took buses to Reserves through all the newly captured = territory and through one of the fiercest storms I have seen.

August 26th = 1918. 1 am.=20 arrived at the reserves and marched 3 miles to the trenches at Cappy. = The=20 shelling was just hell. As soon as we landed we had to go out on lines = till=20 midnight.

August 27th = 1918. 1 am. had=20 to go out on patrol. It was terribly exciting. Had all the patrol killed = or=20 wounded bar the Sergeant and myself. 3 am. After fairly hard fighting we = got=20 forward 2000 or 3000 yards, gaining full observation of the enemy. I was = out on=20 patrol four times during the day. It was great fun getting our artillery = on=20 enemy marks as they would present themselves.

August 28th = 1918. Rained=20 nearly all the morning. 4 am. we advanced after some very heavy = fighting, a=20 further 1000 yards. No prisoners. 9.30 am. Fritz gave us a very heavy=20 bombardment with gas to which we replied with one twice as = heavy.

10 am. we had rather a hard = fight but=20 gained a further couple of woods and a few more hundred yards. My mate = and I had=20 rather a narrow escape. We walked right into a Fritz post. Lines were = terribly=20 troublesome; also a few snipers who had been missed in the advance. = About 3 pm.=20 a sniper put a bullet clean through my tin hat.

4 pm. we went over with a = party of 50 and=20 after some heavy fighting again and heavy machine gun fire, captured the = village=20 of Frise with 70 prisoners, 14 machine guns and 3 Minnewerfers and a = 77mm gun.=20

9 pm. we were relieved and = went back to=20 Cappy. During the day I got a fair dose of mustard gas so tonight have = lost my=20 voice and am nearly blind.

August 29th = 1918. The=20 people who took over from us, advanced 6000 yards last night without any = resistance. At 11 am. we moved up to Dompierre which was our old front = line=20 yesterday.

August 30th = 1918. 1 am.=20 They have had some very heavy fighting, up forward, so I was one of a = party who=20 had to go up for supports and had a very rough time. During the day we = had to=20 retire about 50 yards. 5 pm. We landed back with our unit and then went = for a=20 walk on which we got lost. We were in this predicament till 11.30 pm. = when we=20 found that for the last three hours, we had been wandering no further = than 200=20 yards from our trench. Moved up to Frise landing there about 12.30 pm = and went=20 for a bath in the canal. During the afternoon we were heavily bombed by = enemy=20 planes.

August 31st = 1918. 10 am.=20 moved up to supports via Clery-sur-Somme and Ommiecourt-les-Clery under = full=20 view of enemy balloons. We were terribly shelled and lost heavily in = casualties.=20 We sheltered for some hours under a hill but still received casualties. = 6 pm.=20 went out in charge of patrol of 10 men, four of whom were killed and 3 = wounded.=20 This is the worst patrol I have yet been on. 8 pm. moved up into close = supports.=20 9 pm. went out on patrol again. This time with greater success. 10 pm. = had to go=20 out on lines. These are the curse of my life. Midnight, warned for = another=20 patrol. If these continue, I can see yours truly getting another Blighty = or=20 going under all together. I am fearfully tired. The old Hun has started = a=20 terrible bombardment. During the day I got a further dose of gas so my = voice has=20 gone completely.

September 1st = 1918. 1.30=20 pm. this patrol is fearful. We have been surrounded 100 yards behind = enemy front=20 line. Have had a solid hours fighting. Everyone is wounded, self = included, only=20 a little one through the arm. 2.15 am. still fighting hard. I am afraid = we shall=20 never get out of this lot. Out of our party of 20, 14 are now dead. Just = got our=20 Lewis gun working so have a good chance of getting out now and cutting = our way=20 through to our own lines. 4.30 am. arrived back at our lines in time to = hear we=20 were going over the top at 6 am. 6 am. under way. The enemy is resisting = terribly. They are the best fighters we have struck. 7 am. Casualties = are=20 fearful.

The Division on our right is = suffering=20 heavily. As they are getting wounded, they are falling into the swamps = and=20 drowning. 8 am. held up by machine gun fire and bombs. 8.55 am. just = finished an=20 hours bombing. Casualties are increasing every minute. We are now up = against it=20 and right in front of a big brick wall which is infested with machine = guns.=20

At last it has happened. My = mate and I=20 were sitting in a shallow trench and a 5.9 shell fell in beside us and = wounded=20 us both. I was carried out to the dressing station and from there to a = field=20 ambulance ( 5 kilos ) by four Fritz prisoners. From here we went in a = motor=20 ambulance to Dauors where we were dressed. 8.pm. loaded aboard the = hospital=20 train for Rouen.
